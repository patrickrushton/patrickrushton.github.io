NS Hispeed offers high speed train journeys to national and international destinations. Customer research showed that that users were dissatisfied with a number of aspects of the web siteâ€™s booking tool. The extensive redesign provides a quicker and clearer process by leading the user through the steps for booking their journey and making it much easier for the user to find and compare prices, routes and journey times. The checkout and payment flow is optimised to help the user complete their booking.

Personas & user scenarios

Based on insights from multiple data-points including market research, user interviews and focus-group session recordings, I created a set of key-personas and empathy maps. These were used to get an overall picture of the needs of the users and were helpful to refer to when making design decisions and gaining consensus from the stakeholders.

Concept testing

A number of common user scenario's were picked out to form the basis the the key user journeys. After several creative sessions and whiteboard sketches, an initial concept took shape. A prototype was put together and tested with several different users. The images below are stills from a user testing session. The red lines and circles are from the eye-tracking software which helped identify where the users attention was focussed at a given point in the user journey.

Interaction design

In my role as UX lead for the NS, I was responsible for continued development and improvement of the NS website, mobile app (NS Reisplanner) and the yellow & blue ticket touchscreens. My role was to gather requirements from stakeholders, define user flows and interface designs and present solutions to the client and development teams.
